About Me

Hello! I am Subham Banerjee, a Master's student in Astrophysics at the University of Bonn, and a researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Radio Astronomy. My research focuses on pulsar timing arrays, primordial gravitational waves, and cosmic inflation, particularly their impact on the stochastic gravitational wave background (SGWB).

In my work, I use NANOGrav data to probe inflationary signals, reheating, and early universe physics.

Research

My current research interests include:

  • Pulsar Timing Arrays: Using radio emissions from pulsars to detect gravitational waves.
  • Cosmic Inflation: Investigating the early universe's inflationary phase and its effects on the SGWB.
  • Gravitational Waves: Exploring the primordial gravitational wave background and its role in the evolution of the universe.
  • Nonlinear Dynamics: Studying the application of nonlinear dynamics to galactic simulations.
